Learn to Code Colorado, 2017 December 20

Galvanize Platte

people I met

Talked to Melissa, who recently was laid off from an administrative job. She's considering joining the tech industry. Also, Kyle, who currently works for Graphik, is considering joinging the six-month web development immersive and was asking me about my experience in the program thus far.

what I learned

I learned to work with grid values in CSS, and experimented mixing units (pixels, em, percentage, fractionals). I also got a chance to study code for other websites, paying particular attention to animation properties, such as transform and transition.

questions I have

After talking with some of the other attendees with no background in coding or the tech industry, I started wondering about other approaches to preparing to enter the field: Is it productive to learn the terminology and semantics before actually writing code, or is it more efficient to learn both simultaneously? Could a young child effectively work as a web developer after enough experience? After doing some research and experimenting with animation in CSS, could it ever be as effective or as finely tuned if I don't also incorporate some JavaScript?
